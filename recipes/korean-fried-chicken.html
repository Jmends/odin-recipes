Korean Fried Chicken

Korean fried chicken that is a version of fried chicken found in korean. This recipe makes tender, juicy, and flavorful chicken and it is a must try!

Ingredients

    Marinade:

  • 1 pound skinless,boneless chicken thighs, quarted
  • 1/2 yellow onion, grated
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fine salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground balck pepper

    Other:

  • 4 cups for frying, or as needed

    Batter:

  • 3/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup self-rising flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup very cold water, or as needed

Directions:

  1. Make Marinade: Stir together chicken, onion, garlic, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl until chicken is coated. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight, 4 hours to overnight.
  2. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan to 340 degrees F (171 dgrees C).
  3. While oil is heating, make batter: Whisk together cornstarch, flour, sugar, pepper, and salt in a large bowl. Gradually whisk in cold water until mixture resembles smooth pancake batter. Use tongs to remove chicken from marinade to batter; stir to coat chicken completely. Discard marinade.
  4. Working in batches, fry the chicken in hot oil for 4 minutes. Transfer chicken to a cooling rack.
  5. Increase oil temperature to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  6. Working in batches, fry chicken again in hot oil until golden brown and crispy, 3 to 4 minutes. Transfer to wire rack to drain.
